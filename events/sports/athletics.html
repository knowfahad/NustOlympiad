Athletics

Short Put

Rules
  • Upon Calling the athlete’s name, they have sixty seconds to commence the throwing motion otherwise they are banned from the game.
  • The athlete may not wear gloves.
  • The athlete must rest the shot close to the neck, and keep it tight to the neck throughout the motion.
  • The shot must be released above the height of the shoulder, using only one hand.
  • The athlete may touch the top or outside of the circle or toe board, but must not touch the top or outside of the circle or toe board, or the ground beyond the circle. Limbs may however extend over the lines of the circle in the air.
  • The shot must land in the legal sector (34.92°) of the throwing area.
  • The athlete must leave the throwing circle from the back.

Long Jump

Rules
  • No part of the athlete’s foot should cross the front edge of the foul line.
  • Three attempts would be allowed. Only farthest legal jump would be counted.
  • The distance would be measured from the front edge of the foul line of the first landing point of the athlete.
  • Somersaults would not be permitted.

100 Meters Race | MF

Rules
  • Runners line up in a straight line before the race.
  • Run within your Lane.
  • The first runner whose torso crosses over the finish line is the winner.

200 Meters Race | MF

Rules
  • Runners run in an oval shaped track.
  • The first runner whose torso crosses over the finish line is the winner.

4*100 Meters Relay | MF

Rules
  • All runners should remain in their lanes.
  • The baton must be carried by hands. If it’s dropped the runner can leave the lane to retrieve the baton as long as the recovery doesn’t his/her total running distance.
  • The teammate must receive the baton within a 20-minute area.
  • The first runner whose torso crosses over the finish line is the winner.

4*200 Meters Relay | MF

Rules
  • All runners should remain in their lanes.
  • The baton must be carried by hands. If it’s dropped the runner can leave the lane to retrieve the baton as long as the recovery doesn’t lessen his/her total running distance.
  • The teammate must receive the baton within a 20-minute area.
  • The first runner whose torso crosses over the finish line is the winner.
