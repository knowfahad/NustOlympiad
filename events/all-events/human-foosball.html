Human Foosball

Event Description
  • 5 participants per team.
  • A life-size foosball ‘table’ is to be set up using any kind of support on which 6-8 poles will rest.
  • There should be sufficient space for people to stand, holding the poles and be able to kick the ball within their allotted spaces. Nets/Goals should be present on each side.
  • The set up may be modified/decorated to match any given environmental theme such as:
    1. Recycling + Global warming:
      The ball can be made to look like a wad of trash and each team can try to kick it into their respective goals which would be decorated to look like recycling bins. A large, adjustable thermometer can be placed beside each goal, set initially to full temperature. Every time someone scores a goal, the temperature can be taken down a notch. The first team whose thermometer reaches the optimal temperature/brings down global warming wins. (Awareness may be raised on effects of littering on the environment and the correlation to global warming)
    2. Renewable energy ideas:
      Each team chooses to represent a renewable energy source, kicking the ball which may be decorated to look like a light bulb.
Rules
  • Three rounds will be conducted
  • The matches for first and second round will last for 10 minutes. Teams with maximum goals will move forward to further rounds.
  • The final round will last for 20 minutes and will be played by the top two scoring teams.
Note
  • In case of a draw match, extra time will be given. The prize money will be divided if there is no clear winner.
